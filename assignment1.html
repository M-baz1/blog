1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

As we move to a digitalized society IT systems have become a vital and integral part of every business across every organisations. Across large companies and small startups it systems are being utilised and used in this digital age.

Key roles IT plays in a business:

Communication - Communication Between Employees, Suppliers and Customers. In today’s busy business environment, it is necessary for employees to interact with clients quickly and clearly.

Efficiency of operations - Technology also helps a business understand its cash flow needs and preserve precious resources such as time and physical space. Warehouse inventory technologies let business owners understand how best to manage the storage costs of holding a product.

Business culture- Technology creates a team dynamic within a business because employees at different locations have better interactions. If factory managers can communicate with shipment coordinators at a different location, tensions and distrust are less likely to evolve.

Security -Most businesses of the modern era are subject to security threats and vandalism. Technology can be used to protect financial data, confidential executive decisions and other proprietary information that leads to competitive advantages.


Digital project

A digital project is the creation of something that is digital. Digital projects include : Websites, Mobile apps, Videos, Games, Events, Content, E-commerce, Social media.

Completing a digital project successfully requires, managing online projects from concept to completion, within budget and using a certain amount of resources. It involves planning, delegating, tracking, reviewing, and measuring results.

These are some of the vital skills needed in completing a digital project: Collaborative, Strong communication skills, Problem solver, Attention to detail Strong organizational skill, Ability to multitask and prioritize

Some of the most popular project management methodologies fall into either waterfall or agile.. Waterfall methodologies are a much more traditional approach to project management. Agile is a much more evolved methodology, built on the backbone of flexibility and collaboration.


1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects

Cost risk - A digital project cost usually exceeds the budget set out for it this usually due to poor estimating the initial cost

Schedule risk - the risk that activities will take longer than expected. This will also affect other aspects of the project such as an increase in cost

Performance risk - the risk that the project will fail to produce results consistent with project specifications.

Governance - relates to board and management performance with regard to ethics, community stewardship, and company reputation.

Legal - arising from regulatory obligations, including contract & litigation brought against the organization.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

GDPR is a data protection law that came into practice in may of 2018. The GDPR affects any company that collects or processes the personal data of EU citizens. Under the new rules, individuals have the right to be forgotten, meaning they will be able to request that businesses delete their no longer necessary or accurate personal data. Gdpr will have an impact on all digital projects from Websites, Mobile apps, E-commerce and Social media.if you ha a website privacy policy clearly informs your website visitors that their information is being collected and telling them what type of data is being collected and how long you will keep it. The GDPR requires your site to provide full disclosure of personal information.

To make your website GDPR compliant:

  • The visitors needs to be able to change his/her mind and withdraw consent.
  • The user needs to be able to have all his information properly deleted.
  • All given consent must be recorded as a documentation.
